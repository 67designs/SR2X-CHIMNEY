### Cirrus SR2X Chimney for Various Models of the SR20, SR22/SR22T (G3-G7+) Aircraft


### Overview
This is a complete, production-ready STEP (ISO 10303) 3D model of a **quick-release engine cowling vent** designed specifically for the Cirrus SR20, SR22, and SR22T (G3–G7) with the wide-opening hinge edge.


The vent provides **rapid passive expulsion of trapped residual heat** from the engine bay after shutdown, significantly reducing heat soak damage to magnetos, wiring, hoses, plastics, and other temperature-sensitive components. It also physically blocks birds, rodents, and debris from entering the cowling during the critical cool-down period.


**Repository contains:**
- Full parametric STEP file that can be uploaded to companies such as Shapeways for SLS printing in Nylon 12
- Assembly with elastic cord and toggle grips by the person who utilizes the STEP to produce the part. The elastic cord prevent the oil filler flap from blowing open.


### Key Features & Benefits


- **Effortless Install/Removal** — Single-piece design with two integrated elasticated attachment bands. Fits or removes in seconds with no tools.
- **Lightweight & Durable** — Printed in high-temperature nylon (or equivalent) — strong, impact-resistant, and extremely light.
- **Animal & Debris Barrier** — Securely seals the oil door opening while allowing hot air to escape.
- **Wind-Resistant** — Angled profile and strategic venting geometry prevent lifting or dislodging in wind from any direction. Also prevents the oil flap from slamming shut.
- **High-Temperature Resistant** — Withstands post-shutdown engine bay temperatures during cool-down.
- **Safety Aid** — Highly visible reminder that encourages a proper pre-flight inspection (including oil check). Must be removed before engine start.


### Compatibility
- Cirrus SR20, SR22, SR22T (G3 through G7) with **wide-opening hinge edge**.
- Not compatible with earlier generations or narrow-hinge configurations.


### Important Safety & Usage Notes
- **Ground use only.** Install immediately after engine shutdown.  
- **Remove before engine start** — include in your standard pre-flight checklist.  
- **Remove before checking oil** during pre-flight.  


**This is not a substitute for proper pre-flight procedures.** Always follow your aircraft’s POH and FAA regulations.
